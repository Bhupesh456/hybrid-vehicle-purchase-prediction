# Project Visualizations

This folder contains screenshots from the **Hybrid Vehicle Purchase Prediction** project.

The visualizations summarize the regression analysis and help explain the model's predictions and business insights.

---

# 1. Regression Summary

**File:** `regression_summary.png`

This screenshot shows the Linear Regression output generated using **Microsoft Excel Analysis ToolPak**.

It includes:

- Regression Statistics
- Multiple R
- R Square
- Adjusted R Square
- Standard Error
- ANOVA Table
- Regression Coefficients
- P-values

### Key Findings

- **R² = 0.5837**

  Approximately **58.37%** of the variation in customer purchase behavior is explained by the selected features.

- **Significance F = 0.000**

  The regression model is statistically significant.

- **Income** and **Vehicle Ownership** have the strongest positive influence on hybrid vehicle purchase predictions.

- All predictor variables have statistically significant p-values (p < 0.05), indicating that they contribute meaningfully to the model.

---

# 2. Distribution of Predicted Purchase Percentage

**File:** `prediction_distribution.png`

This histogram illustrates how predicted purchase percentages are distributed across new customers.

### Insights

- Most predicted purchase percentages fall between **50% and 90%**.
- Very few customers have extremely low or extremely high predicted values.
- The distribution suggests a balanced range of customer purchase likelihoods.

This visualization helps identify the overall prediction pattern generated by the model.

---

# 3. Income vs Predicted Purchase

**File:** `income_vs_prediction.png`

This scatter plot shows the relationship between customer income and the predicted likelihood of purchasing a hybrid vehicle.

### Insights

- Customers with higher incomes generally receive higher predicted purchase percentages.
- The positive trend indicates that **Income** is one of the most influential variables in the model.
- Although there is some variation, the overall relationship demonstrates that purchasing likelihood tends to increase as income increases.

---

# Business Interpretation

These visualizations demonstrate how Linear Regression can be used to estimate customer purchase likelihood based on demographic and financial information.

The model can help businesses:

- Identify high-potential customers.
- Support targeted marketing campaigns.
- Improve customer segmentation.
- Enable data-driven decision-making.
